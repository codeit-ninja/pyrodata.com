+++++PROFMAD+++++ I too once made Armstrong mix, though, at the time,I knew no such name. I had recently preaped some Potassium Perchlorate, from some 70% HCl04,(and KOH I presume!). Some days later, I gently ground the salt in a pestle and mortar, happy with very white look of purity it had. I then mused through my chemical cupboard for a suitable reductant. Red Phosphorus caught my eye, and proceeded adding a large spoonful of ~15 gms. Red Phosporus onto the top of ~30 gms. of my KCl04. I then went to gently mix them. WHOOSH &...! Eureka!!! At13 years old, to me this was an invention, as I happily dodged the shower of burning phosphorus that had shot into the air filling my Laboratory (the verandor) with phosporus pentoxide. Mother frowned through the kithchen window at me, shook her head, and went about her duties as normal. 'Mum, Mum, Guess What? DON'T DO IT! An extension to Phosporus / Chlorate pyrotechnics was to dissolve White Phosphorus in Carbon Disulphide. The solution then poured onto a pile of KClO3 and allowed to evaporate. After a few minutes 'the pile' would spontaneously explode with an extremely loud retort.+++++PROFMAD+++++
